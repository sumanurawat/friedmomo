How it works

  1. 1

    Download and open

    Pick your system below, install like any normal app, and open it up.

  2. 2

    Connect once, use forever

    Storyboarder needs access to an AI model to dream up your stories. On your first run, the app walks you through getting a free key from OpenRouter — takes a minute. You pay them a few cents per story; we never see your stories or your money.

  3. 3

    Write your story

    Type a sentence, a paragraph, a premise, or even just a classic title. Watch your storyboard fill in — characters get designed, locations get painted, every panel gets its own image. Then iterate in plain English: "make the wolf scarier", "swap in a rainstorm", "reshoot the whole thing as stop-motion". It just works.

First time opening it? Read this.

Storyboarder is a very new indie app. Your computer doesn't know us yet, so it'll show a warning the first time you open it. This is normal. It only happens once.

On Mac

  1. Double-click the downloaded DMG.
  2. Drag the Storyboarder icon into your Applications folder.
  3. Right-click the app icon in Applications and choose Open. (Double-clicking won't work the very first time.)
  4. You'll see "Apple cannot check this app for malicious software." That's the warning. Click Open.
  5. Done — the app opens. Next time, just double-click like any other app.

On Windows

  1. Run the downloaded installer.
  2. You'll see "Windows protected your PC."
  3. Click More info, then Run anyway.
  4. Follow the installer prompts.
  5. Done — open it from your Start menu or desktop.

On Linux

  1. Download the .AppImage (or the .deb if you prefer).
  2. For AppImage: right-click → Properties → mark as executable. Double-click to run.
  3. For .deb: sudo dpkg -i Storyboarder-*.deb.
